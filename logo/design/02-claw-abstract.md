# Proposal 2: Abstract Claw

## Concept

An abstract interpretation of a "claw" (from OpenClaw) reduced to three sleek, curved lines that converge to a single focal point. The form evokes both a claw grip and a cursor/pointer — reinforcing the idea of the agent reaching into Linear to grab and process issues.

## Visual Description

- **Form**: Three curved lines radiating inward from the upper-left, upper-right, and bottom, converging at a small circle or dot at the center. The curves are smooth and organic but constrained — no freeform swooshes
- **Color palette**: Charcoal (#2d2d2d) for two of the lines, warm amber (#f59e0b) for the third line and the center dot
- **Style**: Flat with subtle line-weight variation (2px vs 3px). The amber element stands out as the "active agent"
- **Composition**: Asymmetric within a circle or rounded square. The claw form occupies roughly 60% of the container, leaving breathing room
- **Details**: The lines have rounded caps. The center dot is slightly larger than the line endpoints to create a focal anchor

## Mood

Precise, intentional, slightly organic within geometric constraint. The amber accent adds warmth and energy without breaking the premium feel.
